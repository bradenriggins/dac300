Certification Domains & Study Aids

Your comprehensive resource center for Texas RDA exam preparation

Tip: The Texas RDA exam covers three main domains. Focus your study time based on your self-assessment results from the previous activity.

1. Jurisprudence (Texas Dental Practice Act)

Understanding Texas dental laws, regulations, and ethical standards is essential for legal practice as an RDA.

Key Topics

  • State Board of Dental Examiners (SBDE) role and authority
  • Scope of practice for dental assistants
  • Delegation and supervision requirements
  • Patient confidentiality and record keeping
  • CE requirements and license renewal
  • Unprofessional conduct and disciplinary actions
Laws & Regulations Ethics Scope of Practice

2. Infection Control

Protecting patients and healthcare workers through proper infection prevention protocols is a core competency.

Key Topics

  • Standard precautions and PPE selection
  • Hand hygiene protocols
  • Instrument processing and sterilization
  • Surface disinfection and barriers
  • Sharps safety and waste management
  • Waterline maintenance
PPE Sterilization OSHA

3. Radiology

Producing diagnostic quality radiographs while minimizing radiation exposure requires technical skill and safety knowledge.

Key Topics

  • X-ray production and image formation
  • Intraoral and extraoral techniques
  • Digital imaging systems
  • Image quality and error correction
  • Radiation safety and ALARA principle
  • Anatomical landmarks interpretation
Technique Safety Digital Imaging

Practice Strategies

Self-Testing Techniques

  • Teach-back method: Explain concepts aloud as if teaching someone else
  • Practice questions: Work through end-of-chapter questions
  • Scenario-based thinking: "What would I do if..."
  • Timed quizzes: Build speed and confidence under time pressure

Hands-On Practice (During Externship)

  • Infection Control: Practice PPE donning/doffing sequence daily
  • Sterilization: Walk through instrument processing steps
  • Radiology: Observe and practice positioning techniques
  • Documentation: Review how records are maintained

Weekly Review Schedule

Consider this study pattern:

  • Monday-Wednesday: Focus on one domain (e.g., Jurisprudence)
  • Thursday-Friday: Focus on second domain (e.g., Infection Control)
  • Weekend: Mixed review and practice questions
